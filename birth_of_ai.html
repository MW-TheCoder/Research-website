The Birth of AI

AI officially began as a field in 1956 during a workshop at Dartmouth College. Important scientists such as John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and Nathaniel Rochester met to explore how machines could mimic human intelligence. This was the first time people came together to set goals for AI and create programs that could solve problems, play games, and even learn. This period gave AI its name and set the stage for major progress in computing.

In 1956, the Dartmouth Workshop brought together researchers like John McCarthy and Marvin Minsky to define AI.

This event officially marked the beginning of AI as a field of study.
