Foundations of AI

The foundations of artificial intelligence began in the 1940s when scientists like Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts created a simple model of how neurons in the brain might work using math and logic. This early work helped people start thinking about how machines could be designed to “think” or process information like a human brain. These ideas laid the groundwork for future research in AI, even though the technology didn’t exist yet to make it a reality.

In 1943, Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts proposed a model of artificial neurons, forming the foundation of AI.

This early work laid the groundwork for neural networks, which later became essential to AI development.
